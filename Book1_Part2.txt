"Step back," she said.

"What?"

"Just—trust me."

Damian's eyes narrowed, but he obeyed. She raised her palm toward the door and focused on the sensation inside her—the cursed energy that had been awakening since the moment she'd set foot in the academy. It wanted out. It wanted to *break.*

She let it.

A wave of force rippled from her hand and slammed into the iron door. The metal groaned, twisted, and then—with a sound like a thunderclap—gave way. The door burst open, revealing a narrow passage lit by flickering torches.

Damian stared at her. "When did you—"

"Later." She grabbed his uninjured arm and pulled him forward. "Move."

---

The passage wound upward through the mountain's ancient roots. Lyra had no idea where she was going—every tunnel looked the same, stone walls etched with runes that hummed faintly beneath her fingertips. The curse guided her, she realized. Or perhaps it was simply leading her deeper into its own web.

They moved in silence, Damian's breath ragged beside her. Every few minutes, she felt the bond pulse—his pain filtering through, sharper now that they were connected. She'd never asked for this. Never wanted to feel his suffering as her own.

*But you wanted him,* the voice whispered. *You wanted his attention, his rivalry, his gaze. This is what it costs.*

"Shut up," she muttered.

"What?" Damian glanced at her.

"Nothing. Keep walking."

The tunnel opened into a larger chamber—crumbling pillars, dust-covered statues, the remnants of a great hall lost to time. And there, huddled beside a broken fountain, were two figures.

"Lyra!" Maris leaped to her feet, her blonde hair tangled, her eyes wide with relief. "We felt the surge—Kade said we had to come find you—"

Kade stood behind her, his jaw tight, a bruise darkening his cheek. "The academy's on lockdown. The council's sealed the east wing. Something's wrong with the seal at the top of the tower—we could feel it from the dorms."

"I broke it," Lyra said flatly. "Or Damian broke it. The ritual—"

"We need to leave." Kade's voice was hard. "Now. Before they realize we're down here."

"How?" Damian's arm hung useless at his side. "Every exit will be guarded."

Maris stepped forward, her hands already glowing with soft green light. "I've been practicing. The herbology masters taught me a bit about concealment spells—nothing impressive, but enough to mask our presence for a short time. If we move quickly—"

She didn't finish. She didn't need to.

Lyra looked at the four of them—her small band of misfits, her found family. Kade, the loyal guardian. Maris, the gentle healer. Damian, the arrogant boy who had become something more complicated.

*Curse-bond,* the voice reminded her. *You are bound to him now, whether you like it or not.*

She didn't answer the voice. Instead, she nodded.

"Let's go."

---

Maris's spell held for exactly eleven minutes.

They crept through servant passages, behind walls, through forgotten corridors that hadn't seen footsteps in decades. Lyra felt the curse humming in her veins, responding to her urgency, warning her of approaching danger before it arrived. Twice, she pulled the others into shadow as guards passed—陌生人 in black robes, their faces hidden, their movements synchronized.

*The Shadow Council,* she realized. *They're moving against us.*

As they reached the eastern stairwell, the concealment finally failed. A guard turned the corner, saw them, and opened his mouth to shout—

Kade moved first. A single strike to the jaw, silent and precise, and the guard crumpled.

"Divide," Lyra heard herself say. "We'll meet at the old gate—the one by the abandoned greenhouse."

"You can't be serious." Damian's voice was sharp. "You're injured, the seal is breaking, and you want to split up?"

"It's not a request." She met his gaze, grey eyes to grey eyes. "We move faster that way. We'll cover more ground, confuse their search."

"I'll go with Lyra," Maris said quietly. "Kade can take Damian."

"I'm fine—"

"You're not." Lyra didn't soften the words. "Your arm is broken, and I can feel your pain through the bond. Don't make me explain what that means."

Damian's jaw tightened, but he didn't argue.

They separated.

---

Lyra and Maris ran.

The greenhouse was a ruin—glass panels shattered, plants dead or dying, the air thick with the smell of decay. Lyra had chosen it for a reason: beneath the dead vines was a passage to the old boundary wall, a gap in the academy's wards that had been overlooked for years.

"Here." Maris pulled back a rotting lattice, revealing a narrow gap in the stone. "This goes out?"

"I think so."

They squeezed through one at a time. Lyra felt the curse pulse as she crossed the threshold—a warning, perhaps, or a test. But nothing happened. They emerged on the other side, gasping, into the cool night air.

The academy's towers rose against the stars. From here, Lyra could see the seal at the highest point—a faint glow, flickering like a dying candle.

"It's cracking," Maris breathed. "Lyra, the seal—"

"I know."

Below them, torchlight moved through the grounds. The search was expanding. They had minutes, perhaps, before the entire academy was locked down.

Kade and Damian appeared from the darkness, moving fast. Kade's face was pale; Damian's arm was bound now, cradled against his chest.

"The gate's guarded," Kade said. "Six guards, full armor. They're not letting anyone out."

"Then we climb." Lyra pointed to the wall—a crumbling stretch of ancient stone that had once marked the academy's outer boundary. Beyond it, the forest waited. Freedom.

"You can't be serious." Damian's voice was incredulous. "That wall is twenty feet high, and you're—"

"I'm faster than I look."

She didn't wait for arguments. She gripped the first handhold and pulled herself up. The stone bit into her palms, but the curse seemed to lend her strength, helped her find holds that shouldn't have been there.

One by one, they followed.

At the top, Lyra paused. She looked back at the academy—the towers, the windows, the glow of the failing seal. Somewhere inside, Selene was watching. Waiting.

*This isn't over,* she thought. *The curse is still inside me. The bond is still there.*

But they were free. For now.

She jumped.

---

They ran until the academy's lights were nothing but a distant glow on the horizon.

In the forest, safe for the moment, Kade built a fire. Maris tended to Damian's arm with quiet efficiency. Lyra sat apart from the others, her knees drawn to her chest, watching the flames.

"So." Kade's voice broke the silence. "What now?"

"Now we figure out how to stop the curse." Lyra's voice was flat. "Before it consumes me. Before it consumes all of us."

Damian looked at her from across the fire. In the flickering light, his face was harder to read than usual. But she felt his worry through the bond—a steady pulse beneath her own exhaustion.

"The library," he said quietly. "There are texts—old texts. In the forbidden section. Something about breaking curses."

"That section is sealed."

"Not for you." His grey eyes held hers. "You heard that voice. You've been hearing it since you arrived. Whatever's happening inside you—it's connected to the academy's origins. Connected to the curse."

Lyra wanted to argue. Wanted to deny it. But the truth was plain: the curse had chosen her for a reason. Or perhaps she'd chosen it.

"We go back," she said slowly. "We find a way to break the bond. And we stop Selene before she uses the seal's collapse to unleash whatever's been locked away."

Silence. Then Kade nodded.

"Then we rest," Maris said softly. "And tomorrow, we plan."

They huddled around the fire, four figures in the darkness, bound together by something stronger than magic. Lyra closed her eyes and let the curse settle inside her—not gone, not dormant, but quiet for now.

Tomorrow, the real fight would begin.

But tonight, they were alive.

Tonight, they were free.

And that was enough.
